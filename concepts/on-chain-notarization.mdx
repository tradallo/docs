title: "On-chain notarization"
description: "Optional Solana memo-program anchoring for tamper-evident, permanent UTR timestamps."

UTRs are signed by Tradallo, which is enough for most consumers — verifying
the signature against the published key set is a single offline check.

For consumers who want a stronger guarantee — *"this trade existed at this
specific moment, and Tradallo can't have backdated it"* — UTRs can also be
**notarized on-chain** by posting their hash to the Solana memo program.

## Why a separate notary layer

Signature verification proves: *Tradallo asserted this UTR is real.*

On-chain notarization proves: *Tradallo asserted this UTR existed before
slot N.*

The second statement is stronger because it's witnessed by an
adversarial-resistant network — not just Tradallo's own infrastructure. A
trader could collude with Tradallo to backdate a signature, but they cannot
collude to backdate a Solana block.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="UTR finalizes">
    A trade closes, the canonical envelope is built, the SHA-256 hash is
    computed, the ed25519 signature is added.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hash queues for notarization">
    If the source account has notarization opted in, the UTR hash is queued.
    The queue runs every five minutes (Vercel cron).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Memo posted to Solana mainnet">
    The cron picks up unnotarized hashes, batches them into one or more memo
    transactions on Solana mainnet via the [SPL Memo Program](https://spl.solana.com/memo),
    and waits for confirmation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Proof recorded">
    Once confirmed, the memo's transaction signature, slot, and posting
    timestamp are stored alongside the UTR in `utr_notarizations`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verifying a notarization

```bash
curl https://www.tradallo.com/api/v1/utrs/<utr_hash>/notarization
```

Returns a signed envelope:

```json
{
  "v": 1,
  "kind": "notarization",
  "utr_hash": "sha256:b81f…",
  "chain": "solana-mainnet",
  "signature": "5VzN…",
  "slot": 287123456,
  "posted_at": "2026-04-30T17:25:00Z",
  "explorer_url": "https://explorer.solana.com/tx/5VzN…",
  "envelope_signature": {
    "alg": "ed25519",
    "key_id": "tradallo-2026-04",
    "value": "MEUCIQDx8…"
  }
}
```

The `explorer_url` gives anyone a one-click way to confirm the memo
transaction exists on Solana, contains the expected hash, and was confirmed
at the expected slot.

## Why Solana

Three reasons:

1. **Memo program is a first-class primitive** — no smart contract to deploy,
   audit, or upgrade. The instruction is `Memo(bytes)`; the bytes are the UTR
   hash. Minimal attack surface.
2. **Sub-second finality** — confirmations land in 1-2 seconds, so the
   notarization is bounded by the cron interval (5 min), not the chain.
3. **Sub-cent cost** — even at scale, posting one memo per UTR is in the
   single-digit-dollar-per-month range. No batching tricks required.

## What it doesn't do

On-chain notarization does **not**:

- Reveal the trade. Only the **hash** is on-chain — the trade itself stays in
  Tradallo's database. An observer of Solana sees a 32-byte hash, not your
  symbol/size/PnL.
- Prove the trade is *correct*. It proves the trade *existed at slot N*. The
  signature on the UTR is what proves Tradallo asserts it's a real trade.
- Make the trade reversible. UTRs are immutable in either direction — once
  the hash is on-chain, removing it is not possible.

<Note>
Opting out of on-chain notarization keeps the trade fully verifiable via the
ed25519 signature. Notarization is a stronger guarantee, not the baseline
guarantee.
</Note>
